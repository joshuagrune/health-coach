# Research Sources

Stable source IDs for citation. Each entry: ID, URL, short annotation.

## SRC001 — Bosquet et al. (2007) — Taper meta-analysis

- **URL:** https://journals.lww.com/acsm-msse/fulltext/2007/08000/effects_of_tapering_on_performance__a.19.aspx
- **Annotation:** Meta-analysis of tapering effects. Optimal strategy: ~2 weeks, 41–60% volume reduction, maintain intensity/frequency.

## SRC002 — Foster (1998) — sRPE, monotony, strain

- **URL:** https://journals.lww.com/acsm-msse/fulltext/1998/07000/monitoring_training_in_athletes__with_reference_to.23.aspx
- **Annotation:** Session-RPE load, monotony (mean/SD of daily load), strain (load × monotony). Used for illness/injury risk monitoring.

## SRC003 — Stöggl & Sperlich (2015) — Intensity distribution

- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4621419/
- **Annotation:** Pyramidal vs polarized training; 3-zone framing; typical "mostly easy" patterns for endurance athletes.

## SRC004 — Gabbett (2016) — Workload spike

- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4789704/
- **Annotation:** Acute:chronic workload ratio; spike risk; chronic load protective when spikes avoided.

## SRC005 — Hulin et al. (2015/2016) — ACWR injury risk

- **URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26511006/
- **Annotation:** High ACWR linked with increased injury risk in team sports.

## SRC006 — Williams et al. (2017) — EWMA vs rolling averages

- **URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28003238/
- **Annotation:** EWMA-based acute/chronic load more sensitive than rolling averages for spike detection.

## SRC007 — Damsted et al. (2019) — Running load change

- **URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30526231/
- **Annotation:** Half-marathon prep; sudden weekly distance changes associated with injury risk.

## SRC008 — Damsted SR (2018) — Load change systematic review

- **URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30534459/
- **Annotation:** Limited but suggestive evidence for load change ↔ injury in running.

## SRC009 — Zouhal et al. (2021) — ACWR controversy

- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8138569/
- **Annotation:** Editorial on ACWR limitations; use as heuristic, not oracle; methodological criticism.

## SRC010 — Salvor API OpenAPI

- **URL:** https://api.salvor.eu/openapi.json
- **Annotation:** Schema for workouts, sleep, activity, vitals, scores; field units; date params; data quality notes.

## Strength Training

### SRC011 — Resistance training dose response (2025)

- **URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41343037/
- **Annotation:** Meta-regression on weekly volume and frequency. Volume matters more for hypertrophy; frequency benefits strength. Diminishing returns at higher doses.

## SRC012 — Schoenfeld et al. (2023) — Resistance training prescription

- **URL:** https://bjsm.bmj.com/content/57/18/1211
- **Annotation:** Systematic review and Bayesian network meta-analysis. Prescription variables for muscle strength and hypertrophy in healthy adults.

## SRC013 — Deloading Delphi consensus (2023)

- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10511399/
- **Annotation:** International Delphi consensus on integrating deloading. Typical: every 5–6 weeks, 6–7 days; reduce volume/sets, maintain frequency.

## SRC014 — Deloading 1-week effects (2024)

- **URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38274324/
- **Annotation:** One-week deload midpoint: similar hypertrophy gains; may reduce lower-body strength vs continuous training.

## Endurance / Zone 2 / Intensity Distribution

## SRC015 — Polarized vs other 2024 meta-analysis

- **URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38717713/
- **Annotation:** Polarized training superior for VO2peak in short interventions (<12 weeks). Similar for time-trial, other metrics.

## SRC016 — Elite endurance training distribution (2023)

- **URL:** https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fspor.2023.1258585/full
- **Annotation:** >91% of sessions involve >60% low intensity. Pyramidal and polarized common; sport-specific variations.

## SRC017 — 92 marathon plans quantitative analysis (2024)

- **URL:** https://sportsmedicine-open.springeropen.com/articles/10.1186/s40798-024-00717-5
- **Annotation:** Pyramidal intensity distribution; ~65% Zone 2–3; ~6% high intensity.

## HIIT / Intervals

## SRC018 — HIIT FITT principle (2025)

- **URL:** https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/physiology/articles/10.3389/fphys.2025.1553846/full
- **Annotation:** Customizing HIIT via FITT. Work-bout and recovery duration critical. 1–2×/week can be effective.

## SRC019 — HIIT self-selected rest (2023)

- **URL:** https://sportsmedicine-open.springeropen.com/articles/10.1186/s40798-023-00601-8
- **Annotation:** Fixed vs self-selected rest equally effective when total rest controlled. Self-selection improves perceived autonomy.

## Flexibility / Mobility

## SRC020 — Static stretching dose meta-analysis (2024)

- **URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39614059/
- **Annotation:** Optimal dose for flexibility. Larger volume improves ROM; effects within 2–4 weeks.

## SRC021 — Stretch vs foam rolling (2024)

- **URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40279-024-02041-0
- **Annotation:** Both increase ROM. Static stretching faster (≤4 weeks); comparable long-term.

## Sleep / Recovery

## SRC022 — Sleep interventions systematic review (2023)

- **URL:** https://ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC10354314/
- **Annotation:** Sleep hygiene, napping, sleep extension improve performance. SWS critical for physical recovery.
